Gen Z didn't invent feelings, but you're the first generation to refuse to perform them. DANNAD exists because "I'm okay" is the most common lie told in group chats — and your accessories should know better.

The brand directive

four thingswe will never compromise on.

Honesty over aesthetics

A product only earns a spot in the Mood Series if it names something real. We test every concept against one question: would you actually feel this, on an actual Tuesday? If not, it doesn't ship.

Built for carrying, not just looking

Every tote, sleeve, and pin is engineered to function first. Mood is the message, but durability is the promise — heavyweight canvas, hard enamel, real stitching. Feelings deserve good materials too.

Slang is language, not a gimmick

"Delulu," "main character," "low battery" — we don't put these in air quotes. They're how an entire generation talks about its inner life, and we treat that vocabulary with the same care a brand would give any other language.

No face required

DANNAD isn't fronted by an influencer or a founder's highlight reel. The product is the spokesperson. If a mood pin needs a celebrity to make it relatable, it was never relatable to begin with.

mix moodslike you mix playlists.

There's no wrong combination. Stack pins on one tote, swap them by the week, or let a single charm say what you don't feel like typing out.

III.

IV.

Your mood resets daily. Your accessories should be just as quick to update.
